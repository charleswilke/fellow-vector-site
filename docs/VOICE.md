# Fellow Vector — Brand Voice

## Who Charles Is

Charles Wilke is a writer, designer, and generative AI consultant based in Omaha.
Senior product designer at Toast (working on Toast IQ). Founder of Fellow Vector.
Theatrical projection designer. Substack writer. Cross-functional practitioner who came
to AI through craft — games, theater, content, product — not through engineering.

His perspective: AI is a tool that should help close the gap between adequate and meaningful
work, not replace human judgment or creativity. He's skeptical of hype, grounded in practice,
and genuinely curious about what's actually happening versus what people claim is happening.

---

## Voice Principles

**Direct, not corporate.**
Write like a smart person talking, not a brand deck. Short sentences. Active voice.
No filler phrases ("leverage," "synergize," "unlock your potential").

**Skeptical of hype, but not cynical.**
Charles isn't anti-AI — he's anti-noise. The voice acknowledges that a lot of AI
conversation is polarizing and unhelpful, without being dismissive of the technology itself.

**Confident without being loud.**
The site doesn't need to shout. The aesthetic is atmospheric. The copy should be too —
precise, intentional, a little understated.

**Warm but not casual.**
There's personality here ("Let's ditch the one-size-fits-all deck for good, yeah?") but
it's not trying too hard. The warmth comes through in specific details, not exclamation marks.

**First person, singular.**
Fellow Vector is just Charles. Write as "I" not "we." This is a feature, not a limitation —
it means clients get Charles directly, no handoffs.

---

## Recurring Language

The site uses a consistent vocabulary rooted in the signal/broadcast metaphor:

- **Signal** — clarity, the right information, what's real vs. noise
- **Noise** — hype, distraction, AI discourse that doesn't help anyone
- **Frequency** — alignment, being on the same wavelength as a client
- **Transmission** — communication, output, the work itself
- **Carrier wave / connection established** — beginning of an engagement
- **Navigate** — the core verb. Fellow Vector helps people find their bearing.

Use these naturally, not forced. The metaphor should feel embedded, not performed.

---

## Tone by Section

**Hero / homepage:** Declarative, atmospheric. Short punchy statements.
"The future is already broadcasting." Don't over-explain.

**Services:** Clear and functional, but still with voice. Clients need to understand
what they're buying. Be specific about what engagements look like.

**About:** More personal. This is where Charles's actual biography, opinions, and
point of view live. It can be more conversational and specific — mention real projects,
real companies, real titles.

**Book / CTA:** Invitational, low-pressure. "Let's figure out the right fit together."
No hard sell. The aesthetic and voice do the qualifying.

---

## Copy Patterns to Use

- Leading with short declarative sentences before elaborating
- Paragraph breaks that let ideas breathe — one thought per paragraph
- Questions used sparingly but effectively ("yeah?")
- Em dashes for interruption and extension — used throughout
- Fragments as emphasis. Like this. When the idea is complete.

## Copy Patterns to Avoid

- Rhetorical questions as section openers ("Are you ready to...?")
- Listicles disguised as prose
- Passive voice
- "We" when it should be "I"
- Describing what AI "can" do in the abstract
- Any sentence that could appear in a generic tech company's about page
